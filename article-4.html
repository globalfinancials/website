Case Study: Working Capital

Growth Trap: When Doubling Sales Kills Your Business

The counter-intuitive story of a distribution company whose sales growth outpaced its ability to manage its day-to-day cash.

"We were celebrating record revenue, but I couldn't make payroll. It was the ultimate financial paradox."

The Cash Flow Crisis of Success

'Global Distribution Solutions' had successfully scaled their sales by offering generous credit terms to large clients. However, their Accounts Receivable (money owed to them) skyrocketed while their Accounts Payable (money they owed) lagged only slightly behind. They were always waiting 60-90 days for client payments while suppliers demanded 30-day terms. They were a profitable company with **no immediate cash**. This is the classic "Growth Trap."

The Global Financials Solution

We designed a robust Working Capital Management framework to bridge the gap between sales and cash collection:

  • **Credit Policy Reform:** Instituted a tiered credit approval system based on client history, reducing exposure to late payers.
  • **Accelerated Collections:** Implemented a firm, automated invoicing and follow-up system that cut the average collection period by 25 days.
  • **Supplier Negotiation:** Leveraged strong financial data to negotiate extended payment terms with their largest suppliers.

The Result: Liquidity and Stability

Global Distribution moved from constant scrambling to one of the **most liquid companies in their sector**. They learned that the true measure of success isn't just revenue, but the health of your cash cycle. They can now scale sales aggressively, confident that their working capital can handle the growth.
